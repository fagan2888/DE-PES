    ABOUT THE ROLE

    • Building and leading a dedicated thermal engineering team
    • Being responsible of the mechanical and thermal design of complex subsystems within the thermal control subsystem
    • Design of the fluid coolant network and thermal components, including heat exchangers, pumps, routing, valves, and other devices
    • Performing literature research and trade-off analysis for the definition of subsystem requirements and create the solutions to meet the expected performance requirements
    • Collaborating with specialists in other disciplines to support their thermal needs and integration into the thermal control subsystem
    • Conducting testing of the designed subsystem, post-data analysis with theoretical and experimental data and writing reports
    • Developing and performing dedicated thermal and structural analysis by means of commercial FEM tools


    WHAT IS REQUIRED?


    Qualifications :

    • Master’s degree in Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering or related engineering field and 5 years of experience OR Ph.D. in Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering in the field of thermal control with 2 years of practical experience
    • Strong knowledge within thermal system designs, from first principles concepts through design of production hardware
    • Understanding of thermal modelling at various levels (1D, 2D and 3D)
    • Good knowledge of Matlab/Simulink and FEM simulation tools
    • Experienced in Project and Team management
    • Good written and oral communication in English. Other languages are considered an asset
    • Strong work ethics and passion to set and meet high performance standards
    • Leadership and team motivation skills
    • Able to set priorities and work under tight deadlines

    Preferred :

    • Knowledge/experience in the Semiconductor, Metallurgy and/or Space industry is an asset
    • Familiarity or experience with multi-phase thermal management technologies (heat pipes, pumped two-phase, vapor compression heat pumps, phase change materials)
    • Familiarity or experience with valves, vacuum systems and flow regulation control
    • Experience with validation and verification of thermal systems using laboratory and in-field testing

    Due to the nature of the Equipment, the person filling this position cannot have any medical implants, such as pacemakers, that are sensitive to strong magnetic fields.

    • Competitive salary package & benefits
    • Enthusiastic and motivated team
    • Friendly and innovation focused working environment
    • Flexibility and responsibility within the team
    • Ability to develop within the team as the company grows
