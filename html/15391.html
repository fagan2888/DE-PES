Role Summary: Within the AvioAero Advanced Technology Organization,The Commercial Engine Performance&Technology sub section is responsible for the definition of the engine Performance&Technology sub section is responsible for the definition of the engine performance, notably steady state,transient&operability, from the initial phace of the New Product Introduction development, post preliminary design, until the engine certification, including ground and Flight testing validation.

Essential Responsibilities

The Transient Performance & Operability Lead
Engineer will join the GE Catalyst team to lead the Transient Performance &
Operability work scope on the assigned projects, notably engine transient
performance analysis, transient model development, engine operability assessment,
model validation by engine ground and flight testing, certification reports. The Transient Performance & Operability Advanced Lead Engineer responsibilities include:
  • Work cross functionally with Controls, Operability, Systems, Aero, Heat Transfer, and hardware design engineers to accurately model and validate engine performance
  • Support efforts to validate and update the engine transient model according to internal practice/standard and test evidence
  • Partake in cross functional teams in preparation of performance audits and program design/technical reviews
  • Effectively communicate, present technical data and provide recommendations to internal and external GE customers
  • Utilize the latest thermodynamic modeling tools to analyze engine transient performance & operability data and provide technical direction to make program decisions
  • Liaise with the Controls organization to define the power management and performance control/limiting schedules
  • Contribute to definition of engine operability requirements (e.g. compressor stability, inclement weather, transient scheduling)
  • Support operability tasks during test validation and engine certification phase
  • Support engine test planning, coverage and data analysis for engine test
  • Provide technical guidance, direction and training to less experienced engineers
  • Share engineering information and promote open dialogue
  • Ensure proper documentation of all work.

Qualifications/Requirements
  • Master's Degree in Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering from an accredited university or college
  • Proven experience in engineering position in aerospace industry in a similar role in Performance, Operability, Transient Analysis and/or Controls
  • Demonstrated experience in engine thermodynamic modeling and test data support and analysis
  • Strong analytical skills and knowledge of thermodynamics and aerodynamics
  • Fluency in English (speaking and writing/reading)
  • EU Work Permit

Desired Characteristics
  • Strong experience in a performance related position
  • Prior Turboprop/Turbofan/Turbojet engine experience
  • Prior engine qualification and/or certification experience
  • Previous experience with industry or GE thermodynamic modeling tools, especially NPSS
  • Familiarity with simulation tools and high-level program languages (C++, MATLAB, FORTRAN etc.)
  • Effective in contributing in a cross-functional teamwork environment
  • In-depth understanding of engineering technology and tools
  • Strong interpersonal and leadership skills
  • Passionate, curious and enthusiastic for engineering challenges
  • Team worker and problem solver
  • Solid oral and written communication skills
