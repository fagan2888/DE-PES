Role Profile And Responsibilities
  • Provide technical support on EHS compliance, and EHS management system projects.
  • Develop EHS compliance procedures, plans, permits, and reports.
  • Design and implement EHS management systems and programs for clients.
  • Perform EHS compliance audits and implement follow-up corrective actions to ensure compliance with local regulations.
  • Assess regulatory compliance across various EHS programs and environmental media.
  • Conduct training, field coaching, and assessment for safety performance improvement projects.
  • Provide on-site EHS compliance support at client facilities.
  • Manage multiple projects within scope/budget/schedule expectations and ensure quality standards on project deliverables.
  • Support ERM Partners-in-Charge and other Project Managers to effectively manage and deliver projects.
  • Actively develop new business and wok with colleagues on proposals;
  • Project Management (e.g. project setup on ERM systems, project management tasks, etc.);
  • Participate in projects including but not limited to EHS and social audits, due diligence assessments, Phase I ESA, regulatory review and advisory services in:
  • Conducting document review;
  • Conducting site visit;
  • Meeting with client and relevant parties; and
  • Writing reports.
    • Keep up to date with EHS regulations locally and international standards;
    • Take other appointments reasonably from Line Manager as needed from time to time.
    • Providing technical and project management expertise to a variety of projects with an emphasis on EHS compliance, due diligence and management systems auditing.
    • Ensuring that projects are planned and effectively executed, so that they meet ERM’s and our client’s expectations;
    • Maintaining and developing client relationships, preparing proposals and presentations, and supporting marketing and sales initiatives.
    • EHS due diligence for acquisitions, divestitures, mergers, joint ventures, capital raising and project finance including Equator Principle reviews
    • Analysis for bankruptcy, reserves, provisioning, Asset Retirement Obligations, and financial reporting
    • Carbon and energy due diligence
    • Post-merger Integration support
    • Management system, EHS culture, gap analysis, and benchmarking
    • Embedding social, stakeholder and sustainability elements into due diligence

    Requirements

  • Degree qualified in Environmental Engineering, Health & Safety or other relevant studies, preferably a Masters Degree level or equivalent.
  • 6+ years professional experience in EHS management or consultancy, preferably with exposure in a range of industries ideally including chemical, mining, infrastructure, project finance and manufacturing sector experience.
  • Recognised EHS auditing qualification and/or experience.
  • Expert in technical trouble shooting and regulatory requirements.
  • M&A or consulting experience is preferred
  • Proven project management experience, preferably including multi discipline and multi-site projects.
  • Good top management and client interface capability and open to travel and limited secondment opportunities;
  • Ability to multi-task, maintain flexibility, proactive and fast learner, excellent communication skills and good team worker
  • Good command of written and spoken both in Japanese and English
