Maintenance Operation Engineer

Norwegian Gatwick, GB

1 week ago

The Technical Department in Norwegian is part of the Continuing Airworthiness Management Organisation responsible for the 787 fleet.

The department is looking for a Maintenance Operations Engineer to join the team based in Gatwick, reporting to the Maintenance Operation Manager.

Primary Responsibilities And Duties
  • Control and oversight of subcontracted continuing airworthiness activities performed by approved EASA Part M & Part 145 organisations, in accordance with EASA Part M Subpart G
  • Active control monitoring of deferred defects, scheduled tasks, and work packs for maintenance inputs and engine changes ensuring rectification within deadlines
  • Utilising and acting on data trends provided by the engine health and aircraft health monitoring systems
  • Checking that all performed maintenance is verified according to approved procedures with reference to the NUK CAME, technical procedures manual and maintenance manuals
  • Ensure that records and documentations are completed and updated in the Maintenance Engineering Management MRO system
  • Follow up interface between contractors and sub-contractors (Part-145/Part-M/Part-21)
  • Attend daily meetings with the MRO and subcontracted organisations
  • Management of customer furnished parts and materials, maintaining adequate stock levels throughout the network
  • Liaising with OEM’s to increase reliability of their products and reduce removal rate/deferred defect rate
  • Reviewing supplier contracts to ensure agreed service levels are met and product performance is within the contracted criteria
  • Additional work according to CAME and Company needs


Qualifications/Requirements
  • Relevant Engineering Degree or Aircraft Engineer to B1/B2 standard, with a minimum of 5 years post qualification experience in a technical role in commercial aviation
  • Flexible attitude, team spirit, able to manage different levels of workload
  • Strong analytical capability with excellent MS office suite skills & MRO systems
  • Awareness of material planning principles
  • Ability to develop and maintain good working relationships with supplier’s at all functional levels.
  • Proven ability to achieve goals while working under tight time constraints while maintaining technical standards and integrity


We offer
  • Competitive company benefits including ID tickets
  • Good pension and insurance schemes
  • Positive and exciting place to work in a global organisation
  • Challenging, variable and dynamic environment
