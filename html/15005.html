Data Scientist at GSI Investigations (apply till 21.10.2019)

Swedbank Estonia Estonia

3 weeks ago 40 applicants

Collaborating with anti-financial crime experts in a fast-paced interdisciplinary and international team to contribute with data-driven insights into anti-financial crime measures – would this make your eyes shine? If yes, this might be the challenge for you – we are now looking for a Data Scientist in our GSI Investigations team.


GSI Investigations (GSII) is the advanced investigative and anti-financial crime expertise arm of the Anti-Financial Crime Group function. The mission of the unit is to minimize potential or occurring reputational and monetary losses to Swedbank derived from financial crime and fraudulent activities. Our main goal is to increase trustworthiness and transparency of the business environment by Anti-Money Laundering, Countering Financing of Terrorism, Anti-Fraud and Evasion of International Sanctions related activities. More concretely - transactions monitoring, screening, in-depth analysis of fraudulent schemes and activities as well as reporting to competent authorities.


What will be your challenge?

As a Data Scientist, you are expected to engage with other GSII team members and provide analytical insights to anti-financial crime measures, participating in a range of activities from monitoring tools development and fine-tuning to supporting with case investigations. This role requires from you a mind-set of being persistent problem solver , a curiosity about data, a natural inclination for the creative and innovative, as well as, fluency in communicating the result of your work.

In this role, you will work in tandem with other GSII data scientists to facilitate developing data science and analytics solutions for GSII; and coordinating activities with related departments in data, analytics, and IT systems related projects. You should be comfortable extracting , cleaning and wrangling large structured and unstructured data sets, exploring and mining them for insights, building analysis pipelines and automating the “boring stuff” in a collaborative environment.


As you will be working in an interdisciplinary team and are expected to take initiative in helping and communicating your field expertise to colleagues with both technical and non-technical background and be able to work across the business with various subject matter experts. You and your fellow data scientists in GSII will be providing a data-driven perspective to solving anti-financial crime-related problems . Besides fluency in database querying and coding for data analysis and reproducible research , your everyday tasks will also require a drive for learning and pro-activeness in identifying potential problems and solutions. You should take pride in translating data-driven insights into actionable next steps and communicating these to an interdisciplinary audience in a coherent manner (both in writing and verbally).


What would help you succeed in the role?

You are creative and proactive and have solid coding skills for data analysis and reproducible research ( Python/R, SQL, Git ). As a person you can take ownership/responsibility for your working area, you are analytical , structured and thorough - able to pay attention to details . You have good communication and cooperation skills (also fluent in English) . You have a relevant university degree (e.g. in computer science, math, physics, statistics, bioinformatics, engineering or similar) or equivalent experience in data and analytics related areas. You want to develop yourself continuously, are open-minded and able to adapt to changes. As a member of an interdisciplinary team you are confident representing the following competencies:


  • Computer science fundamentals;
  • Mathematical fluency and statistics fundamentals;
  • Data search and management (including database querying/ SQL );
  • Programming in high-level scripting languages (preferably Python or R );
  • Data visualization and reporting;
  • Statistical modeling and optimization fundamentals.


A huge bonus would be:
  • Experience with Data Warehousing and/or Data Lake systems;
  • Experience with Natural Language Processing (NLP) and/or Social Networks Analysis (SNA);
  • Knowledge of anti-money laundering, anti-fraud or similar topic(s);
  • Knowledge of the financial industry;
  • Knowledge in project management basics.


Why join us?

Anti-financial crime (AML/CTF/IS/Anti-Fraud) area has received increasing limelight in recent years. The framework and regulations are increasingly complex, which means that the area is developing fast and there is always something new happening which makes our work versatile and challenging one. Being part of the GSI Investigations team you will be in the center of the action, working side-by-side with highly motivated anti-financial crime experts and providing a data-driven perspective to designing solutions for tackling problems in the area of anti-financial crime. Merged in an interdisciplinary team you will have a great and supportive learning environment for acquiring a wide range of relevant background knowledge and skills. And after a while, you are expected to support others in developing in the areas of your core competencies as well.


Want to know more?

In case of questions about the position or recruitment do not hesitate to contact: Recruitment Specialist Kairi Kaur (kairi.kaur@swedbank.ee).
