ispace Europe is seeking a highly motivated and talented developer to implement the company’s Mission Planning Tools and Planetary Data Center.

About Ispace

ispace is a private lunar exploration company whose vision is to expand and sustain humanity’s presence in space by utilizing resources available on the Moon. ispace was a Google Lunar XPRIZE finalist and its 4kg SORATO rover won the XPRIZE mobility milestone award. In addition to rovers, ispace is developing lunar landers with two missions already scheduled for launch in 2020 and 2021.

ispace Europe is the company’s regional office located in Luxembourg, in the heart of Europe. We are developing the next rover missions that will prospect for lunar water and thereby set the first milestone toward lunar ISRU. ispace Europe core engineering competences are in lunar science, rover navigation, mission planning, data processing, and mission systems engineering. We are a diverse and growing team, motivated by the challenges of the Lunar Frontier, and determined to achieve the company’s vision. We rely on every engineer to exhibit a cross-functional mindset, intellectual curiosity, willingness to bridge gaps, appreciation for process, and understanding of the system two levels up and two levels down. The candidate must be self-driven, continuously self-improving, and proud of their work.

Role Description

As a Data Engineer, you will be responsible for creating the suite of tools and environments used to process, analyze, and interpret data collected from our lunar missions. You will work with our mission scientists to create brand new tools for selecting landing sites, our rover engineers to enable efficient surface operations, and our business developers to scale these tools into their own outward-facing services.

Key Responsibilities
  • Work with mission scientists and rover engineers to create the first planetary data processing pipelines and the infrastructure to support them
  • Design and implement the first set of scalable tools for planning lunar missions
  • Test, verify and validate all your products, collect user feedback, iterate
  • Research and stay up to date with state of the art tools and data processing methods used in the planetary science, terrestrial mining, remote sensing, and media industry
Required Skills and Qualifications
  • BS/MS in computer sciences, engineering or related field, or equivalent industry experience
  • 3 to 5 years of experience in software development
  • Knowledge or ability to quickly ramp up with machine learning and other data science methods
  • Experience in documenting data definitions and writing reports in a business-friendly language
  • Exhibit strong intellectual curiosity about the science, engineering, and business disciplines adjacent to your domain of expertise
  • Be highly organized and capable of planning and executing complex technical work
  • Thrive in multi-disciplinary environment in close cooperation with science, engineering and business teams
  • Must have excellent interpersonal, organizational, and communication skills
  • Feel comfortable working on underconstrained problems
Preferred Experience
  • Building, maintaining, and monitoring cloud services
  • Experience with computer vision and GIS are a plus
