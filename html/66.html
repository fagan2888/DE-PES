Summary

This role is a great opportunity to further enhance your career within IT for a leading charitable organisation.

The National Trust continues to make £multi-million investments to transform our technology capability in support of our cause. That’s no small task when you consider the incredible breadth of what we do. We strive to deliver powerful infrastructural changes with an ambitious technological roadmap. This journey of transformation will deliver phenomenal benefits for our organisation and dramatically enhance how we connect people with our cause. We’ve just put a brand new 10 year IT strategy in place that’s firmly aligned with our business strategy – a strategy which will bring big delivery challenges as well as one huge opportunity for your career.

What it's like to work here

We’re bigger than you think, we’re more complicated than we appear and we’re larger scale than you’d imagine. We’ve got passionate people in all our teams, and we’ve got so much more we want to achieve. We’re for ever, for everyone and we really mean that. We don’t want to stop at 5.5 million members and we want everyone to feel welcome and enjoy access to our places, so we need to reach new audiences in ways that are meaningful and relevant to them without losing the hearts and minds of those that are already with us.

Our central head office Heelis, is situation in Swindon, Wiltshire. We're well placed, with ample parking nearby, a shopping outlet with plenty of options for places to eat, and the town centre only a 10 minute walk away. Alternatively you can enjoy our open plan atrium space for lunch times, with our internal café available every day.

What you'll be doing

An exciting challenge to further develop your career. In this critical role, you’ll be part of the driving force behind the delivery of our technology roadmap. You will assist in the technical requirement, design, implementation and support of new systems and services, whilst keeping our charitable cause front of mind.

A technical role model and a valued voice across the Trust; people will look to you for technology expertise and innovative solutions. You’ll manage change brilliantly, establishing best practice, keeping communication levels high and making sure people feel as motivated and inspired as you do. And at the heart of everything you do will be a passion for our cause and a desire to make a positive difference through impactful technology.

You will be involved in a wide variety of work from assessing key technical requirements and research potential solutions, to the implementation and transition into service. In addition, you will be responsible for assisting the technical lead in documenting the High-Level Design and technical requirements for internal and external stakeholders.

Who we're looking for

Skills

Coming to us with enthusiasm and drive, you’ll bring the following skills, knowledge and experience

An evangelist of technology for positive social impact, who already has a keen interest to keep abreast of the latest technologies, whether that be infrastructure, cloud hosting, networks or security. Someone ambitious and driven, who’s ready to step up to this rewarding career challenge.
  • Good communication skills, written and verbal including the ability to communicate with people with varying levels of technical knowledge.
  • Good people and excellent customer service skills enabling strong relationships to be built and maintained across the National Trust.
  • Experience of working on projects including formal tracking and reporting.
  • Knowledge of the Microsoft Office 365 and Azure platform.
  • Strong knowledge of multiple operating systems such as: Windows 7, 10, Server 2012/16/19, macOS 10.13+ and Android 9.
  • The ability to support with application packaging/management and distribution via SCCM and Intune.
  • An understanding of Microsoft tools such as SCCM, Intune, on-premise Active Directory and Azure Active Directory.
  • An understanding of the TOGAF and ITIL frameworks.
  • Previous experience of a technical support background.
  • Experience in preparing technical solutions options and design documentation.
  • The drive and impact to lead change throughout the organisation, managing conflicting demands and overcoming any challenges.
The package

Benefits

Click here to find out more about the benefits we offer to support you.

Benefits include flexible working whenever possible plus free parking at most locations. You’ll be entitled to discounts in high street stores and cinemas, National Trust shops and NT cafes, and have free entry to NT properties for you, a guest and your children (under 18).

Your health and wellbeing is important to us and is supported through generous annual leave and the option to buy additional days (minimum contract length applies), a cycle to work scheme, subsidised health cash plan, a loan towards rental deposits and confidential access to a free support service 24 hours a day should you need it.

Your future financial health is helped by an employer matched - up to 10% of basic salary - contributory pension scheme and you can further your career with training and development tailored to you.

All of this and the opportunity to give something back to your community with up to 5 days of paid volunteering per year !
