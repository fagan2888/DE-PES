Summary

If you are an innovative problem-solver, if you would like to learn something new, if you enjoy and are not afraid of challenges, and have a soft spot for travel – then, this is the job for you!

As a Travel Analyst, you will be the second level of support to our customers. You will work independently on tickets with no phone calls except if you deem them necessary. Your customers will be managing Concur for their companies. They are already trained on the software and will request your help with any anomalies in the system.

You will become a technical resource professional with a great chance to specialize in your given area.

Role
  • You will assist customers regarding misconfigurations and bugs such as discount fares, vendors, locations not visible in the system, or error messages preventing travel bookings.
  • You will work with GDS (Global Distribution System) including Amadeus, Galileo, and Sabre.
  • You will assist your team with operational tasks: case triage; escalation emails; quality assessment, and validation cases going to our development team.
  • You will train customers on basic product functionality.
  • You will engage with other departments to resolve customer issues and escalate to specialist teams when is needed.

Sounds demanding? Don’t worry!
  • You will have dedicated time with your supervisor, mentor, and shadowing sessions to reach your full potential
  • You will get all the trainings and tools needed to perform in your role
  • You will be working under minimal supervision but your colleagues will be ready to help you.
  • You will be in a team where the focus is on behavior rather than numbers.
  • You will be in an environment where you will have the feeling of belonging, of feeling valued, and where your development matters

Role Requirements
  • Good Customer Service skills
  • Troubleshooting and analytical skills.
  • Strong written and verbal skills.
  • Travel industry experience is a plus but not a must!
  • Working knowledge of a GDS is a plus but not a must!
  • Fluency in English is a must, French and German on top are a plus!
