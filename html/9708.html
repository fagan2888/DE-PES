    Our client is a global advertising, analytics and attribution platform - helping the world’s biggest brands and media agencies use real-life data to gain insight on what consumers are doing now, deliver personalised messaging across devices and measure engagement against sales uplift.


    Headquartered in London with over 30 offices globally, our client is a market leader in their industry and has been ranked one of the best companies to work for in the UK. Join a vibrant office with flexible working hours and all the best of tech company benefits including complimentary healthy food and wellness packages.


    AS A PRINCIPAL DATA ENGINEER YOU'LL BE:


    • improving algorithmic technologies and optimising machine learning algorithms / pipelines
    • working alongisde data scientists and a team of engineers
    • owning the design & build of large scale batch/streaming data-processing products (think Spark, Storm) and ETL pipelines
    • participating in an on-call rota to support our systems in production
    • identify & deliver innovative opportunities for long term commercial and technical growth

    ABOUT YOU:

    5+ years advanced level knowledge and experience in:


    • solving complex, large-scale data challenges such (Hadoop, Hive, Presto)
    • Python or Scala for data-intensive use cases
    • orchestration frameworks, like Luigi, Airflow, or Oozie
    • data analytics and ingestion engineering
    • streaming data, relational and non-relational databases, distributed processing technologies (Spark, Hive, or similar operations at both the code and config levels)
    • cloud-based services (AWS, GCP)
    • server-side Linux
    • integrating and consuming data from third-party APIs

    IT WOULD BE GREAT IF YOU HAVE:

    Operational familiarity and practical experience with:


    • Cassandra, HBase, or similar data stores
    • PostgreSQL (or MySQL, RedShift), including partitioning and indexing
    • AWS Athena or Google BigQuery to solve data-centric problems
    • complex algorithms and statistical techniques for large data structures
    • Java and JVM optimization
