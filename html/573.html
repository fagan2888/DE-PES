JOB CONTEXT

Neste is part of the Finnish parent company Neste. The Refinery in Rotterdam produces Neste renewable products, using a patented catalyzing technique. The refinery is part of the Renewable Products business area.

Comparable refineries are located in Singapore and in Finland. The Dutch refinery focuses on safe, compliant and sustainable production and continuous improvement projects. Research & Development, Supply Chain, Sales and other staff divisions are steered on a corporate and business area level.

Apart from the manufacturing related functions, the Rotterdam plant houses a number of staff functions important for Dutch operations, i.e. Human Resource Management, HSEQ and Business Management.

The plant, situated on the Maasvlakte, has a production volume of approximately 1200 KTA. It has approximately 120 employees on a regular contract, apart from those employed on an incidental basis. Non core services are outsourced to approximately 60 contractors.

JOB PURPOSE

Developing process control systems and optimising their performance (availability and reliability), on the one hand by reducing failures and on the other hand, by improving maintenance.

RESULT EXPECTATIONS/ FUNCTIONAL ACTIVITIES

Result areas

Core activities

Result criteria

Control, data management and process safety systems implemented
  • discussing process design and regulation strategies for processes and installations with the Process Engineer (with regard to process control and safety of installations)
  • developing process design requirements in the control system, discussing apparatus/software possibilities with suppliers
  • providing interfaces with other modular integrated operating systems for data input and output
  • entering relevant parameters into the system, programming the configuration
  • design, updation and adjustment of the visualisation of regulations for the control room
  • quality of execution with respect to compliance with the programme of requirements
  • extent to which it is possible to make the production process more efficient and safe

Maintenance plans and management system developed
  • developing maintenance concepts on the basis of failure analyses, expected replacements, testing requirements in collaboration with concerned parties, entering the concepts into the system
  • identifying and analysing potentially dangerous situations
  • developing system management concepts (inspection lists, status measurement lists, etc.) and discussing these with managers and supervisors
  • drawing up cost budgets for replacement/modification of the systems
  • making suggestions for modifying the maintenance interval and/or maintenance programme on the basis of the information collected and analysed
  • comprehensive view of maintenance needs
  • quality of plans drawn up
  • extent to which the plans contribute to limiting failures

Functioning control, data management and process safety systems
  • localisation of failures via tests and studies, determining the cause of the failure
  • carrying out maintenance works with regard to process control
  • managing maintenance contracts with regard to the hardware, involving external parties for the maintenance of the hardware
  • commissioning and handing over systems to production
  • concrete diagnosis of failures, in this case of the causes of failures
  • speed and reliability of the solutions for failures

Insight into feasibility of modifications
  • assessing the feasibility of process (safety) modifications, factory extensions and engineering requests from the point of view of process control, in cooperation with the Technical Department
  • submitting project proposals regarding modifications of systems within the process control network
  • advising the management specifically with regard to regulation techniques and process safety aspects
  • extent to which the defined programme of requirements is complied with

Understanding of systems
  • transferring process control knowledge with respect to process hazard evaluations reviews within projects and multifunctional teams
  • advising the management specifically with regard to regulation techniques and process safety aspects
  • advice with regard to operations for optimising the operational process, based on knowledge of and insight into regulation strategies
  • drawing up regulations and instructions with regard to operation and maintenance, and advising the Maintenance Manager on the necessary level of stocks for spares (with regard to one's own field of work)
  • the necessary insights required for the execution
  • quality of the recommendations
  • usefulness and clarity of instructions

WORK-RELATED OBJECTIONS
