Overall Department Direction
As a warship support partner to the Royal Navy, Babcock is a provider of deep maintenance, in-service maintenance and operational support for UK warships. The Warships Technical Authority (WTA) is a multi-disciplined organisation within the Warships Business Unit. The WTA provides roles across a range of engineering, safety, environmental, and supportability disciplines, providing bodies of evidence to influence complex warships through life asset management.

You will be working alongside other highly qualified and knowledgeable team members delivering design and supportability capability whilst providing essential technical support to the Warship growth programme.

Main Purpose of Role
The overriding aim of the Data Engineer is to bring data together from across the Warship community to enable a more data driven approach to be taken within all aspects of Warship through life support, enabling better support decisions to be made.

Working with individual data owners to deliver support analysis, studies, and improvement recommendations, the Data Engineer will work within the Supportability Team within the WTA. This is a new and exciting function in the WTA, and will provide the opportunity for the Data Engineer to glean an excellent understanding of Warship ‘through life support’ and provide opportunities for future career progression.

Required Knowledge, Skills, Experience And Competences

Ideally this Data Engineer will be experienced in data analysis, transformation and visualisation and will have experience of using MS Power BI for data visualisation purposes and will also have MS Excel.

This role is accountable for the identification, and sourcing of data to enable performance, cost, and supportability analysis to be undertaken within the Warships Business Unit.

A knowledge of warships through life support and design management processes would be advantageous, but is not essential.

An excellent communicator, you will be able to articulate complex arguments to a wide range of stakeholders. In particular, you will need to be able to write reports. You will also need to demonstrate that you can drive projects and issues to completion.

Qualifications

Minimum of HNC (or equivalent) preferable and member of relevant institution.
Ideally experienced / interest in data analysis, transformation and visualisation; e.g. MS Excel, MS Power BI.

Location
This role is based at Devonport Royal Dockyard which is located in the vibrant city of Plymouth, Devon in the south west of England. Plymouth is a city with a long and proud military history and more than its fair share of heritage. There is something for everyone here; from a superb coastline if you are a water sports enthusiast or Dartmoor National Park if you enjoy hiking and climbing, or simply enjoy the fantastic scenery. Business Area- Devonport
Babcock International Group owns and operates the main refit, maintenance and workshop area of the 330 acre naval site at Devonport, known as Devonport Royal Dockyard, where it employs thousands of people. The remainder of the site at Devonport is owned by the Ministry of Defence and is known as Her Majesty’s Naval Base (HMNB) Devonport – one of the largest naval bases in Western Europe.
Babcock and the Ministry of Defence work in partnership to operate and maintain this co-located site by providing through-life support for submarines, surface ships and associated systems and equipment.

Babcock has a large design and technology capability providing a wide range of support activities including feasibility and project definition studies, detail design and draughting, development trials, noise and vibration analysis, structural analysis, project management, integrated logistic support and risk management.
