    • Role

    • Expertise in quantitative analysis, data mining, and the presentation of data to understand fraud, and how to prevent it.
    • Build, maintain, and tune advanced predictive models to understand the impact of specific fraudulent behaviors, trends and patterns.
    • Work closely with cross-functional teams of data and backend engineers, analysts, user researchers, product managers
    • Build and analyze dashboards, reports and key datasets to empower operational and exploratory analysis of fraud.
    • Offline analysis of customer data to tune rules, exposes patterns, research anomalies, reduce false positives, and build executive and project-level report
    • Communicate data-informed insights and recommendations to key stakeholders, engineering and product partners
    • Qualifications
    • At least 5 years of knowledge and experience with regard to fraud case management, predictive modeling and analysis, predictive software development
    • An MS/PhD in CS, Machine Learning, Operational research, Statistics or in a highly quantitative field. PhD strongly preferred.
    • Knowledge of cybercrime – account takeover, Card not Present (CNP) fraud, promotion abuse, social engineering etc.
    • Previous experience working in fraud detection and prevention, with an understanding of the impact that has on other areas in the company where business and product decisions are made.
    • Coding skills for analytics and data manipulation (SQL, R, Python, SAS), practical understanding of SQL and NoSQL databases
    • Knowledge of machine learning algorithms including classifiers, clustering algorithms, and anomaly detection a plus
    • Strong analytical and statistical analysis skills in order to extract insights and recommendations
    • Strong communication and data presentation skills
    • Fluent in English with strong communication skills (both written and verbal)
