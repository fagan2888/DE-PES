Data Analyst - Laboratory Technologist


Are you looking for a Data Analyst position where you can hone your primary skill set as well challenge yourself to grow professionally? Do you want to work for an established company where your insights have immediate effects on the results? Are you a self-starter interested in affecting people’s lives? If so, may have just the opportunity for you!


As the Data Analyst, you’ll be responsible for DNA sequencing and MALD-TOF data analysis, data interpretation, report generation/review, along with assisting in daily sample preparation and communicating with customers.


This role will impact customer satisfaction and the success of the company through effective handling, processing and reporting of customer samples in a cGMP-compliant and professional manner to ensure ongoing customer satisfaction.


Responsibilities include but are not limited to :

  • Conduct microbial identification data analysis
  • Assemble .ab1 files for consensus sequence data using DNA assembly software
  • Evaluate DNA sequencing sample data, forms identification conclusions, generates and signs/verifies reports
  • Evaluate MALDI-TOF sample data, forms identification conclusions, generate and sign/verify reports
  • Perform standard microbiology procedures including handling, growth and purification of cultures
  • Perform microbial identification procedures including receipt of samples, DNA extraction, PCR amplification, sequencing, perform required data entry, and run associated equipment (e.g. sequencers, MALDI-TOF)
  • Write, review, and/or edit SOPs, test cases and quality documents for cGMP lab
  • Verify sample reports and send to Customers


Requirements:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Life Science or Biotechnology
  • At least 3 years of experience in a laboratory environment
  • At least 2 years of experience with analyzing DNA sequencing data, including assembly of .ab1 files into a consensus sequence
  • Technical expertise in laboratory functions and services, including microbiology lab techniques and DNA sequencing
  • Computer skills – all Office software and specialized software including DNA sequencing
  • Experience in a 21 CFR part 211 environment
  • Technical training in scientific equipment (e.g. From Applied Biosystems, Bruker)
  • Experience with ISO 17025 (preferable) or ISO 9002 lab environment


Desired Knowledge:

  • Technical training in scientific equipment (e.g. From Applied Biosystems, Bruker) desired
  • Technical expertise in laboratory functions, services and regulations, including MALDI-TOF, FAME, taxonomy, cGMP/GLP, ISO 17025 or ISO 9002 desired
  • Computer skills - MALDI-TOF system microbial identification analysis desired


Benefits:

Plus, they work to maintain the best possible environment for their employees, where people can learn and grow with the company. They strive to provide a collaborative environment where each person feels encouraged to contribute to our processes, decisions, planning and culture.
