    Recruitment Supervisor at Genesis Biotechnology Group

    • PharmOptima, a member of Genesis Biotechnology Group, is currently looking for a Biolanalytical Research Scientist to support activities within bioanalytical lab, including method development, assay validation, sample analysis, reporting, and sponsor interaction. Experience in a Contract Research Organization (CRO) with PK experience, and work in a regulated environment a plus.

    PharmOptima is a CRO providing small and large molecule analytical services along with In-life study support. The bioanalytical group offers GLP and Discovery analytical support, using HPLC-MS/MS instrumentation.

    Essential Functions:

    • Designing, following, and providing input for Study Protocols, development and validation of quantitative methodology utilizing analytical techniques, and final interpretation and reporting of data.
    • The successful incumbent will have experience developing and implementing assays for small molecules. Must have excellent laboratory bench skills, good interpersonal skills, and experience with multiple HPLC-MS/MS platforms is a plus.
    • Particular emphasis will be given to the development of high-capacity-high throughout quantitative LC-MS/MS analytical techniques utilizing validated hardware and software systems. Demonstrated ability to operate independently with experience in the development of rapid HPLC separations and sample extraction techniques to enhance delivery time of analytical data are important.
    • Interfacing with multiple groups for the successful implementation, conduct, and interpretation of studies.
    • Perform bioanalytical validations and qualifications, and regulated and discovery bioanalytical studies.
    • Writing final reports, communicating progress and project issues to management and Sponsors.


    Additional Responsibilities:

    • Good written and oral communication, and organizational skills are mandatory.

    Job Qualifications: General Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities (KSA’s) required

    • A scientific degree (B.S., M.S., Ph.D.) with relevant industry experience strongly preferred.
    • Experience with or knowledge of Microsoft Office, Analyst, Xcalibur software. Experience using Phoenix WinNonlin is desirable.
    • Basic knowledge of the principles of pharmacokinetics (PK), toxicokinetics (TK), and associated in vivo study design is required.
    • Experience with regulated studies including FDA GLP, GMP and GCP requirements and guidance preferred.
    • Ability and experience developing and implementing bioanalytical extraction and instrumentation assays is desirable.
    • Experience with Sciex and Thermo Fisher HPLC-MS/MS (including experience with HPLC-TOF) is preferred.
    • Strong written, presentation and verbal communication skills.


    Physical Demands: Physical, Mental and Workplace Environment Conditions

    • Attention to detail regarding safety and accuracy of procedures performed.

    Working Conditions: Workplace Environment Conditions

    • Position functions are performed in an office and laboratory environment which includes multiple hours of sitting and standing and repetitive hand motions.

    Education and Experience:

    • Position requires a minimum of a Bachelors’s degree (chemistry, biochemistry, pharmacy or a related discipline is desirable), plus applicable experience.
